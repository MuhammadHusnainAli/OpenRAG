# OpenRAG

**OpenRAG is a self-hosted platform for chatting with your documents and building your own AI agents on top of them.**

Upload your files, ask questions in plain language, and get answers drawn directly from your own content with the sources cited. Everything runs on your own infrastructure, so your documents stay with you.

---

## What you can do

### Chat with your documents
Start a conversation, upload one or more documents into it, and ask anything. OpenRAG reads across all of them to answer your questions, and shows you exactly where each answer came from. You can keep multiple conversations, revisit them anytime, and pick up where you left off.

### Build custom agents
Go beyond one-off chats and create your own agents. Each agent has its own **knowledge base** — the documents you give it — and its own personality and instructions. Once it's ready, anyone you allow can chat with it and get answers grounded in that knowledge.

### Deploy in test or live mode
Try an agent privately in **test mode** while you refine it. When you're happy, take it **live**. Updating an agent later — new documents, new instructions — simply creates a new version, and the newest live version automatically becomes the one people use.

### Version history
Every change to an agent is saved as a version. You can look back through the history to see what changed and when — whether you added knowledge or adjusted its instructions — and move between versions whenever you need to.

### Share on your terms
Keep an agent private to yourself, share it with specific people, or open it up publicly with a shareable link. Public agents can be protected with an access key, so only people with the key can use them.

---

## Who it's for

Anyone who has a collection of documents — research, policies, manuals, notes, knowledge bases — and wants a faster way to find answers in them, or wants to turn that knowledge into a shareable assistant. Because OpenRAG is self-hosted, it's a good fit for teams and individuals who care about keeping their data private.
